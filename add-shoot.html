About this page

This is your personal dashboard for managing your own listings on Who Shot Me? - add, edit, or delete shoots, and attach gallery links, all without needing spreadsheet access.

Meet the creator

Hi, I'm Peter.

I'm a keen motorcyclist and GIS professional from the Peak District. I first got into riding on a red 2010 Honda CB125, and these days you'll usually find me out on an orange Honda CB500F with a pillion passenger… a Charmander soft toy. It's for the kids… honest!

After seeing the same question appear time and time again in motorcycle Facebook groups - "Who was the photographer at Cat & Fiddle?", "Does anyone know who was at Snake Pass today?" - I realised there had to be a better way.

That's what inspired me to create WhoShotMe.

The aim is simple: make it easy for riders to find photographers, and help photographers reach more riders. By bringing both together in one place, I hope to save riders from endless searching while giving photographers another way to showcase their work.

WhoShotMe is an independent project that I develop in my spare time, and it's continually evolving based on feedback from the motorcycle community. Every suggestion helps shape the platform and where it goes next.

Thanks for visiting, and I hope you find WhoShotMe useful.

Built by a rider, for riders and photographers.

Your data

We store your name, contact email, logo and website links, and every shoot (location, description, times) and gallery link you submit here. We also keep a simple count of your popup views, gallery clicks, and page link clicks from the public map - shown to you above, visible only via your own personal link.

We keep this data for as long as you're listed, and delete it once you ask us to.

"My location" and "Use current location": if you use either, your browser asks your permission directly - that's between you and your browser, not something we control. If you allow it, your coordinates are used only to show a dot on the map or set the pin, right there on your device; they're never sent to us, stored, or logged anywhere.

This runs on Google Sheets/Apps Script (data storage), Netlify (hosting), the OS Data Hub, Esri, and OpenStreetMap (map tiles - whichever basemap you've got selected), and postcodes.io / OpenStreetMap's Nominatim (turning a typed postcode or place name into a map location) - each processes the minimum needed to make their part work.

Please keep in mind

Only submit content you have the rights to post, and keep listings accurate - no illegal or offensive content. Your link is effectively a password: anyone who has it can edit your listings, so don't share it.

Questions, or want your data removed entirely? Email us.
